01
The Maestro's Manor

A home shaped by music and nature

The manor in Lusławice was not merely a residence — it was the living expression of Krzysztof Penderecki's inner world. Every room told a story: the study where symphonies were born in the quiet hours before dawn, the library lined with scores and letters from fellow composers, the winter garden where rare orchids bloomed alongside sketches of unfinished works.

02
The Park

A living collection from across the world

For over fifty years, Penderecki cultivated a botanical garden of extraordinary breadth. He sought out rare trees — ancient ginkgos, dawn redwoods, the towering Caucasian wingnut — and gave them permanent home in the gentle hills of southern Poland. Walking through the park is to walk through both natural and personal history.

03
The Music Salon

Thirty works that changed music

From the radical sonic experiments of the Threnody to the devotional grandeur of the St. Luke Passion, Penderecki's catalogue spans decades and contradictions. The Music Salon invites you to listen — not just to recordings, but to the stories behind each composition, the conflicts and inspirations that shaped every note.

04
The Amphitheatre

Music beneath the open sky

The amphitheatre carved into the hillside of the Lusławice estate has been the stage for some of Poland's most memorable outdoor concerts. Surrounded by forest, under the wide Małopolska sky, audiences have gathered here each summer to hear great music in the place where it was imagined.

05
The Labyrinth

Find what is hidden within

Somewhere within the green corridors of the labyrinth, four fragments of music await. Each discovery unlocks a moment from Penderecki's life — a memory, a confession, a creative breakthrough. The labyrinth does not give up its secrets easily. But those who persist will find something that stays with them.

06
The Studio

Where compositions took shape

The studio in Lusławice was more than a workspace — it was the crucible in which Penderecki refined and recorded his most ambitious works. Rare archival recordings, manuscript sketches and the testimony of engineers and musicians who worked alongside him reveal a composer of extraordinary rigour and instinct.

07
The Garden of Memory

Remembering Krzysztof Penderecki

Penderecki passed away on 29 March 2020, leaving behind a garden, a body of music and countless lives he had touched. The Garden of Memory collects the testimonies of conductors, students, neighbours and strangers — all united by an encounter with an extraordinary man and the world he created in Lusławice.
